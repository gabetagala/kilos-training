## Key rules

- Rehab ends, first ramp set begins — the 15-min rehab block IS the warm-up; never duplicate it, never add another.
- Queue, not calendar: A → B → C → repeat, whenever you train. A missed day slides the queue; a missed week IS the deload. Nothing is ever made up.
- One spine-loaded lift per session, always first, straight sets, ≥2:00 rest, front squat capped at 2–4 RIR forever. Density formats (supersets/drops/EMOMs) live only on cables, DBs, bands, and bodyweight.
- The spine never moves under load — including pickups: DBs live at knee height on the safeties, every pickup is a braced hinge, floor-press exits are a roll to the side, and any symptom is a hard stop.
- Double progression everywhere: all sets at the top of the range at the listed RIR → smallest jump → bottom of the range. Stall twice on a lift → that lift drops 10% and rebuilds.
- Time valve: past ~32 min entering the last block, cut it to 2 rounds — cut rounds, never whole blocks. Chaos day = heavy slot + first superset (~15 min) at −10%; it counts.
- Shoe lift on for ALL standing/split-stance loaded work; right (smaller) side always goes first and the left matches its reps.
- Fat loss is the kitchen's job: 300–500 kcal/day deficit, 1.6–2.2 g/kg protein, 7–10k steps, 2-min Sunday waist/weight check — never add cardio to out-eat food.

# DENSITY 40 — FINAL — "Armored V-Taper"

**3 rotating sessions (A → B → C) + optional Engine day (D). Honest 35–39 min per session after your 15-min rehab block. Total garage time ≈ 50–55 min including rehab — plan for that, not for 40.**

Your daily rehab block (dead hang → McGill Big 3 → glute bridges) **is** the warm-up. **Rehab ends → first ramp set begins.** Nothing in here repeats it. No other warm-up or stretching exists.

---

## IRON RULES (read once — they run everything)

1. **The spine transmits force; it never moves under load.** Zero loaded flexion, zero sit-ups/crunches, zero kipping (all pull-ups are STRICT — backpack loading, not DB-between-ankles), zero high-rep barbell cycling. Ever.
2. **One spine-relevant heavy lift per session, always first, always straight sets, ≥2:00 rest, never supersetted, never ground out.** All density formats (supersets, drop sets, EMOM-style finishers) live ONLY on cables, DBs, bands, and bodyweight.
3. **Manual-handling rule (the invisible risk):** every single pickup — DBs, plates, the bar, your son — is a mini squat/hinge with a braced neutral spine. Park the DBs on a box or the rack safeties at knee height between sets, not the floor. DB floor press: sit with DBs on thighs, roll back as one unit; to get up, lower DBs to your sides and **roll to your side** — never a loaded sit-up.
4. **Morning rule:** no heavy axial or hinge loading within ~2 h of waking. Dawn-only window? Sessions A and C are fine straight after the full rehab block (low axial cost). If the queue serves **B** pre-dawn: swap the front squat to heavy DB split squats (zero penalty), or simply do the next upper session today and let B land on your next later-in-the-day slot.
5. **Shoe lift ON for ALL standing or split-stance loaded work — no exceptions.** Carries, laterals, curls, calf raises, band work included.
6. **Asymmetry rule:** all unilateral work starts with the **right (smaller) side first; the left matches the right's reps** — never the reverse.
7. **Symptoms = hard stop** on that exercise. Finish the session with walking and light supported cable work only (not carries). Retest next session. Two flare-ups in a row on the same lift → run its listed swap for the rest of the block. A flare **week** → deload protocol (below), which also swaps the front squat → split squats and drops all carries that week.
8. **Extension guard:** "ribs down, glutes on" applies to the landmine press, overhead triceps extensions, flys, lateral-raise drop sets, and every push-up — a push-up set **ends at the first hip-sag rep**.
9. **Log every working set.** Last session's numbers are this session's targets. Rep PRs are the scoreboard.

## THE QUEUE (a queue, not a calendar)

Do the **next session in the queue** whenever you train: **A → B → C → repeat.** Target 3/week; insert **D (Engine)** anywhere on a 4-day week. A missed day slides the queue — nothing is ever broken, skipped, or made up. A missed **week** counts as your deload; resume where the queue left off.

**Hinge Day coordination (one rule):** your rehab app's Hinge Day (RDL 3×8 + single-leg bridges + stretches, 3×/wk) pairs freely with Sessions A or C or rest days. If the queue serves **B (front squat)** on a Hinge-Day morning: preferred — do the next upper session today and run B tomorrow; otherwise run B with the split-squat swap. Do Hinge-Day RDLs 2 h+ after waking too.

## SESSION A — PULL: width, arms, grip (~35–37 min)

*Ramp: 1 easy set of 3–5 pull-ups (or light pulldown ×8). Not logged.*

**A1 · Weighted STRICT Pull-Up — 4×5–8 @1–2 RIR · rest 2:00 · STRAIGHT SETS**
Full dead-hang stretch at the bottom of every rep, zero swing. Load via backpack + small plates. Below 4×5 clean at bodyweight? Run heavy lat pulldown 4×8–10 until you own 4×8 strict, then load and restart at 4×5.

**A2 · Superset ×3 (~45 s between moves)** — cable + DB, no equipment conflict
- **A2a Standing 1-Arm Low-Cable Row** — split stance, slight hip hinge, spine long, free hand braced on thigh/rack, zero torso swing, no loaded forward reach at the stretch. 8–12/side, right side first. @1–2 RIR.
- **A2b DB Lateral Raise** — 12–15 @0–1 RIR. **Final set: drop ~30%, rep out once.** Ribs down on the drop.

**A3 · Superset ×3 (~45 s between moves)** — cable + DB
- **A3a Rope Pushdown** — 10–15 @0–1 RIR.
- **A3b DB Hammer Curl** — 10–12 @1–2 RIR (brachioradialis = the visible forearm mass). Strict, no lean-back.

**A4 · Finisher — 2 rounds (~5 min), untimed, form-gated:**
Suitcase Carry right 35–45 s → left 35–45 s → DB Reverse Wrist Curl ×15–20 → rest 30–45 s.
**Carry rules (apply everywhere):** pick up from **knee height** (rack safeties or box), braced hinge; full stop + re-brace + slow wide turn at each end of your lane; set it down the instant posture or grip degrades — the clock never wins. Load = a weight you could hold ~15–20 s longer than the set.

## SESSION B — LEGS + DELTS + FOREARM BLOCK (~36–39 min)

*Ramp: empty bar ×5 → ~50% ×3 → ~80% of work weight ×2 (~4 min incl. loading). Only barbell configuration today — no mid-session re-rigging.*

**B1 · Front Squat — 4×4–6 @2–4 RIR (hard cap) · rest 2:00–2:30 · STRAIGHT SETS**
Clean grip or cross-arm. Shoe lift on, collars on. **Depth governor (do once, week 1):** film a side view, find the deepest depth where your neutral spine holds (no butt-wink), set the rack safeties there — squat to just above the pins, never below. Bail = dump forward onto the safeties. Bar speed stays crisp; a slow or position-breaking rep ends the set. Explosive intent up on every rep — that's your power work.
**Permanent zero-penalty swap (any day or forever): Heavy DB Split Squat 4×6–8/leg, right leg first.** Near-identical leg stimulus at about half the axial load.

**B2 · Rear-Foot-Elevated Split Squat (foot on sturdy box/stair ~20–30 cm; bench later) — 3×8–10/leg, right first · @1–2 RIR · ~45–60 s between legs/sets.** DBs at sides (doubles as grip). *Optional only if you enter this block under ~20 min: hold the same DBs for 2×15–20 standing calf raises — calves are otherwise a conscious cut.*

**B3 · Superset ×3 (~45 s between moves)** — DB + cable/band
- **B3a DB Lateral Raise** — 12–20 @0–1 RIR. **Final set: drop ~30%, rep out once.**
- **B3b Rope Face Pull** (or band pull-apart) — 15–20 @1–2 RIR.

**B4 · "Popeye" Forearm Block — 3 rounds (~6–7 min), NOT optional:**
DB Wrist Curl ×15–20 → DB Reverse Wrist Curl ×15–20 → Band Lateral Raise ×15–20 → rest 30–40 s. (Same light DBs for both wrist moves — no re-dialing. Kneeling, forearms on thighs or a plate stack; forearms-on-bench later.)

**No carries on Session B** — the week's axial compression is deliberately spread out.

## SESSION C — PUSH + CHEST + CARRIES (~35–38 min)

*Setup: bar on the LOW rack safeties (floor-press height) — only barbell configuration today. Power primer: 2×3–5 explosive push-ups (hands on safeties or floor, ribs down). Ramp: ~50% ×5 → ~80% ×2.*

**C1 · Heavy Press — 4×6–10 @1–2 RIR · rest 2:00 · STRAIGHT SETS**
Interim: **Barbell Floor Press** off the low safeties, dead-stop each rep — solo-safe by design (or DB floor press with the roll-to-side rule). Moderate arch only, ribs down.
**The day the bench arrives, this slot becomes 30° Incline DB Press 4×6–10** — start ~20–25% under your floor press and rebuild.

**C2 · Superset ×3 (~45 s between moves)** — cable + bodyweight
- **C2a Lat Pulldown** — overhand, just outside shoulders, full stretch + 1-s pause at top. 8–12 @1–2 RIR.
- **C2b Feet-Elevated Push-Up** (feet on plates/low box — upper-chest bias) — 10–15 @1–2 RIR; add a backpack plate once you pass 15. Set ends at the first sagging rep.

**C3 · Superset ×3 (~45 s between moves)** — band + DB
- **C3a Low-to-High Band Fly** (band under the rack foot; bilateral for time — single-arm low-cable fly is the rotation option) — 12–15 @0–1 RIR. Ribs down, no lean-back.
- **C3b DB Supinated Curl** — 8–12 @1–2 RIR, strict.

**C4 · Overhead Rope Triceps Extension — 3×10–15 @0–1 RIR · ~40 s rest.** Facing away from the low pulley, elbows by ears, **ribs down, glutes on** — no lumbar arch.

**C5 · Finisher — Farmer Carries: 3 × 35–45 s · rest 60–75 s.** Both DBs, all carry rules from A4 apply (knee-height pickup, stop-and-turn, posture ends the set). Untimed — no EMOM clock.

## SESSION D (optional 4th day) — ENGINE · 20–30 min
Pick one, guilt-free to skip on 3-day weeks:
- **Engine EMOM 16–20** (4-min cycle ×4–5): min 1 — band-resisted march anchored to the rack, 40 s hard · min 2 — push-ups ×8–12 (sag rule applies) · min 3 — lat pulldown or band row ×12–15 · min 4 — farmer carry ~35–40 s (knee-height pickup). Spine-neutral, breathing hard — this is the WOD replacement.
- **Zone-2:** 25–30 min brisk incline/stroller walk, "can talk, don't want to" pace.

**Standing weekly item regardless of D:** one 25–30-min zone-2 stroller/hill walk on any off day, plus 7–10k steps daily. That's the aerobic base for playing with your son; it costs zero gym time.

## PROGRESSION (runs solo for months)

- **Starting loads (week 1 = calibration pass):** pick a load that leaves ~3–4 RIR at the BOTTOM of the rep range. When in doubt, lighter — PRs start on pass two.
- **Double progression everywhere:** hold the load until ALL sets hit the TOP of the range at the listed RIR → next session add the smallest jump you own (+2.5 kg barbell/backpack · one DB click · next band) → restart at the bottom of the range. On the front squat, the load-jump-plus-rep-reset counts as the single allowed change; it stays ≥2 RIR forever, bar speed is the gate.
- **Effort tiers (only three):** heavy slots 1–2 RIR (**front squat 2–4, hard cap**) · superset compounds 1–2 · isolations 0–1. When in doubt, add a rep, not a kilo.
- **Stall (definition):** two consecutive sessions on the same lift with no new rep and no new load → drop that lift ~10% (round down to loadable), rebuild through the range. That's it — a stall never triggers a whole-week deload by itself.
- **Carries:** when every carry ends tall with grip to spare → add time up to 45 s, then +2.5 kg and reset to 35 s. Log load + time like any lift. As your Hinge-Day RDLs progress pain-free over months, pickups may move from knee height toward mid-shin — that is your progressive hinge lane for real life (car seats, strollers, a growing kid).
- **RIR calibration:** every 4–6 weeks, take the FINAL set of pushdowns (or a seated supported curl) to true failure to re-anchor the feel. Never on the front squat or any barbell lift.
- **Rehab-lane progression (in that app, not here):** when Hinge-Day RDLs feel ~6/10 and crisp for two straight weeks → +2.5 kg there.
- **Accessory rotation (every 4–6 weeks, optional):** swap within families only — laterals (DB ↔ cable ↔ band) · curls (hammer ↔ reverse ↔ supinated ↔ Bayesian) · triceps (pushdown ↔ overhead) · row (1-arm cable ↔ chest-supported DB — if you rotate in the DB row, pair it with BAND laterals to avoid re-dialing) · fly (band ↔ single-arm cable) · carries (farmer ↔ suitcase). New pick starts at the bottom of its range. The three anchors — pull-up, front squat, press — never rotate; their PRs are the novelty.

## TIME VALVE + FALLBACKS

- **Time valve:** past ~32 min entering the last block → cut it to 2 rounds. Past ~35 → 1 round. Cut ROUNDS, never whole blocks — B4 (forearms) always gets at least 2 rounds.
- **Chaos-day bare minimum (~15 min):** heavy slot + first superset. Sleep-wrecked? Run the heavy slot at −10%. It counts, log it, the queue advances.
- **Deload (reactive only, no calendar):** triggered by a back flare OR a newborn hell-week: that week, half the sets at ~80% load (3–4 RIR), front squat → split squats, no carries. A chaos week of missed sessions already WAS the deload — no makeup, no guilt.

## BENCH-ARRIVAL UPGRADE MAP

| Now (no bench) | After the bench |
|---|---|
| Barbell/DB floor press (C1) | 30° Incline DB Press (start ~20–25% lighter) |
| Standing 1-arm low-cable row | May rotate to Chest-Supported DB Row, 30–45° (pair with band laterals) |
| Split squat rear foot on box | Rear-foot-elevated on bench |
| Kneeling wrist curls | Forearms-on-bench wrist curls |
| — | Optional block rotation: Barbell Hip Thrust 3×8–12 may replace B2 for a 4–6-wk block (ribs down, finish with glutes, roll the bar on/off while seated against the bench — never sit up under load) |

## WHERE THE VOLUME LANDS (per A→B→C pass ≈ one week at 3/wk)

Lats/upper back **10 sets** (+3 face pulls) · side delts **9 direct + 2 drops ≈ 11 effective** · chest **10** (interim upper-chest ≈ 6 until the bench — stated honestly) · triceps **6 direct** + all pressing · biceps **6 direct** + all pulling · **forearms 8 direct + 3 hammer + 7 carry sets + daily rehab hangs** · quads **7** (1×/pass frequency — a conscious trade) · glutes: squats + splits + Hinge Day + carries · hamstrings: Hinge Day only (**maintenance dose, on purpose**) · calves: optional 2 sets (conscious cut). Every priority muscle sits at 8–13 sets where growth-per-minute is steepest.

## FAT LOSS (goal #2 is won in the kitchen)

**300–500 kcal/day deficit · 1.6–2.2 g/kg protein · 7–10k steps/day.** Training preserves the muscle; it doesn't out-burn food — never add cardio to out-eat anything. **Sunday check-in (2 min):** waist at navel + the week's average scale weight. Two weeks with no downward trend → add 1–2k daily steps first, then trim ~150 kcal.

## 8-WEEK OUTLOOK

- **Wk 1–2 (calibration):** find every starting load at 3–4 RIR bottom-of-range; film the front squat once and set the safety-pin depth; establish carry baselines; start Sunday check-ins. No PR chasing yet.
- **Wk 3–4 (progression proper):** first load jumps and rep PRs; bench likely arrives → run the upgrade map (incline DB starts light and rebuilds); one RIR-calibration failure set on pushdowns around wk 4.
- **Wk 5–6:** first stalls appear → micro-reset rule (−10%, rebuild past the wall in 3–4 sessions); first accessory-rotation window opens if you're bored — anchors stay put.
- **Wk 7–8:** a chaos week will probably have happened — it counted as the deload; reassess at wk 8: waist trend, front-squat and pull-up PRs, photos. If RDLs have been crisp for weeks, nudge them +2.5 kg and consider mid-shin carry pickups. Then keep running the queue — the program doesn't expire.

## Why this design (synthesis rationale)

Skeleton: **athletic-density (DENSITY 40)** — the highest combined audit score (7.5/8/8.5) and the best psychological fit (one pristine heavy lift, then everything on density formats = CrossFit feel without barbell cycling). Grafted from **v-taper-builder**: right-side-first asymmetry rule, universal shoe-lift rule, the mandatory forearm block, and tiered RIR by spinal cost. Grafted from **minimalist-strength**: the chaos-day −10% adjustment, the two-flare-up swap escalation, explicit ramp protocols, the RDL-progression handshake with the rehab app, and honest "conscious cut" volume framing.

Audit findings that drove structural changes: (1) **Carry pickups were the #1 hidden spine risk in all three drafts** → carries are now form-gated timed sets (never true EMOMs), picked up from knee height off rack safeties with a braced-hinge cue, with a load governor (~15–20 s reserve), stop-and-turn lane rules, and a logged progression; the barbell suitcase carry from pins was deleted outright. (2) **Floor seated cable row = loaded flexion for a tight-hamstringed lifter** → replaced with a standing split-stance 1-arm cable row; chest-supported row returns only post-bench. (3) **One-barbell re-rig and adjustable-DB re-dial time bombs** → every session now uses exactly ONE barbell configuration, and no superset ever pairs DB with DB or cable with cable; that's why B (front squat day) carries no barbell press and no carries (which also fixes the axial-compression stacking flagged on old Session B). (4) **Hinge Day collisions** → one executable rule: Hinge Day pairs with upper sessions; B slides or swaps. (5) **Morning-rule dead end for a pre-dawn dad** → A/C are cleared for early training; only B adapts. (6) **Manual handling (DB kick-ups, plate loading, getting off the floor)** → its own Iron Rule. (7) **Time claims** → recomputed honestly at 35–39 min with a written time valve (cut rounds, never blocks; forearms protected) and the total garage time (~50–55 min incl. rehab) stated. (8) **Goal-coverage gaps** → triceps doubled to 6 direct sets, side delts raised to ~9 direct + drops via the band-lateral slot in the forearm block, a supinated curl slot added, interim upper-chest honestly labeled, a tiny power primer (explosive push-ups + explosive squat intent) added, and the zone-2 walk made a standing weekly item. (9) **Progression ambiguities** → stall defined (two sessions, no new rep/load → −10% on that lift only, distinct from the reactive week deload), starting-load protocol added, RIR collapsed to three tiers, front-squat one-variable contradiction resolved. Rejected ideas: minimalist's top-set/back-off percentage math (produces non-loadable numbers and needs a calculator), the barbell floor glute bridge as a heavy slot (fiddliest setup, hyperextension risk, second barbell config — glutes are amply covered by squats, splits, Hinge Day, and carries; hip thrust returns as an optional post-bench block), calf raises as default (conscious cut), and the 4-session queue (thinner per-muscle frequency at a realistic 3 days/week).
