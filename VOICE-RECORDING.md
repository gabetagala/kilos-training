# Your voice as the coach — recording guide

The player already prefers your recorded clips over the system voice: drop
files into `public/voice/` named `<slug>.m4a` (or `.mp3`) and they take over
instantly — any phrase without a clip falls back to the system voice, so you
can record in one sitting or gradually.

## How to record (2 minutes, iPhone Voice Memos is fine)

1. Quiet room, phone ~20cm from your mouth, speak like a calm coach — firm,
   not shouty (quiet confidence).
2. Record ONE memo: say each phrase below once, with a ~1 second pause
   between phrases. If you fumble one, just say it again — I'll take the best
   take when slicing.
3. Drop the file (or files) into `~/Downloads/kilos training/` like the art,
   tell Claude, and it gets sliced, trimmed, normalized, and installed.

## The 23 phrases (in recording order)

Say them with the intent in brackets — don't read the brackets.

1. **"Get set"** — [something new is coming, heads up]
2. **"Go"** — [start now, punchy]
3. **"Hold"** — [steady, calm]
4. **"Rest"** — [release, exhale]
5. **"Switch sides"**
6. **"Left side"**
7. **"Right side"**
8. **"Your pace"** — [it's a lifting set, take your time]
9. **"Warm up"**
10. **"Lift"** — [bridge going up]
11. **"Squeeze"** — [top of the bridge, 2-second squeeze]
12. **"Lower"** — [slow down-phase]
13. **"One"**
14. **"Two"**
15. **"Three"**
16. **"Four"**
17. **"Five"**
18. **"Six"**
19. **"Seven"**
20. **"Eight"**
21. **"Nine"**
22. **"Ten"**
23. **"Session complete. Nice work."** — [the payoff line]

Numbers are reused everywhere: rep counts on the McGill holds AND the 3-2-1
countdowns, so give "three", "two", "one" a little countdown energy.
